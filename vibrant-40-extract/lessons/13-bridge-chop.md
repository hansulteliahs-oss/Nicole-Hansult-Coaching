## Heading
Bridge Chop for Core & Glute Strength

## Body
This move combines a classic glute bridge with a controlled chop motion, challenging your core stability, shoulder strength, and glute activation, all in one powerful move. It's especially effective for creating cross-body integration and building foundational strength for everyday movements.

**How to Do It:**

**Set-Up:**

Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor, hip-width apart.

**Bridge:**

Lift your toes, press through your heels, and lift your hips toward the ceiling, forming a straight line from shoulders to knees.

Engage your glutes and draw your navel toward your spine to maintain the lifted position.

**Chop:**

While holding the bridge, extend arms straight above your chest. Lock your elbows. The movement is coming from your shoulders.

In a quick and controlled motion, move your arms in a "chop" motion.

**Focus on Form:**

Keep hips lifted and steady throughout. Hip bones should have the same distance to the ceiling or to the floor - in other words, keep your hips stable.

Your head and shoulders stay grounded.

Don't forget to breathe.

**Targeted Muscle Groups:**

Glutes (from the bridge position)

Hamstrings

Deep layers of your abdominal muscles and obliques (from the chopping motion)

Shoulders and arms (for stability during the movement)

**Repetitions:**

While breathing slow and controlled, silently count 15 chops.

Repeat for five rounds, increasing by 5 chops each round, resting briefly between sets.

Prioritize form and stability over speed and reps.

**Why It Matters:**

This move builds the kind of strength that helps you move better in daily life, especially when bending, twisting, or lifting. It's also a great way to wake up your core and strengthen your backside, which can improve posture and reduce low back discomfort.
