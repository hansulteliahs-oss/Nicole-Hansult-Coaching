## Body
Let's be clear: I love sweets. And I don't believe you need to give them up to live a healthy life.

But here's the difference: I don't eat dessert mindlessly or treat it like an all-or-nothing event. I enjoy a few bites, something rich and satisfying, and then move on. It's not about restriction, it's about intention.

In many European cultures, sweets aren't the enemy. They're savored, not devoured.

**Here's how to enjoy sweets mindfully**

Choose quality over quantity. Skip the processed junk and go for darker chocolate, fruit-based desserts, or small pastries made with real ingredients. The darker the chocolate the more health benefits (antioxidants and fewer calories).

Slow down. Eat dessert at the table, mindfully, not in front of the TV or while scrolling your phone.

Savor the experience. Let each bite be enough. The first bite can be the most satisfying. You don't need to finish it all to feel satisfied.

Keep sweets occasional, not constant. If it's always available, it becomes background noise, not a treat.

Plan for the day. I know that I'll enjoy a dessert with my coffee to keep me going and lift and my spirits. Just pick when you want to relish the sweetness. Will it be at lunch? Or after your dinner?

There are healthy desserts (like a square of dark chocolate or a homemade dessert made with Greek yogurt and fruit) and there are times to not deny yourself. It doesn't have to be everyday, but find what's manageable where you can feel good about embracing your sweet tooth but not be consumed by it.

Remember: It's not about being perfect. It's about enjoying life, including dessert, in a way that supports your health, your goals, and your joy.
