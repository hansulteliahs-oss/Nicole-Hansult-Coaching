## Heading
Dead Bug (Core Stability Focus)

## Body
**How to Do It:**

Lie on your back with your arms extended straight up toward the ceiling and your knees bent at 90 degrees (shins parallel to the floor).

Brace your core as if you're gently pressing your lower back into the mat (or ground).

Slowly lower your right arm and left leg toward the floor, keeping them just above the ground.

Pause briefly, then return to the starting position.

Repeat on the opposite side.

**Important Cues:**

Keep your back flat against the floor throughout the movement.

Move with control. Speed is not the goal here.

If your back arches, reduce your range of motion.

**Targeted Muscle Groups:**

Transverse abdominis (deep core)

Rectus abdominis (six-pack muscle)

Obliques

Hip flexors

Shoulder stabilizers

**Recommended Repetitions:**

Beginner: 6 reps per side (12 total), 2 sets

Intermediate: 8–10 reps per side, 2–3 sets

Rest: 30–45 seconds between sets

**Why It Matters:**

This movement is foundational for building core stability and reducing lower back pain. Unlike traditional ab exercises, it teaches your body to maintain alignment under movement, essential for daily activities like walking, lifting, or even getting out of bed.

This is a low-impact, high-benefit way to restore strength, improve posture, and protect the spine.
