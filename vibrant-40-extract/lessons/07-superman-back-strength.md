## Heading
Superman Back Strength: Build a Stronger Back

## Body
One of my favorite foundational strength moves, the Superman Exercise, strengthens your core (specifically your lower back and glutes) as well as hamstrings, which are crucial for posture, balance, and injury prevention as we age.

This move helps counteract the effects of sitting and slouching. It's also a safe, no-equipment way to build body awareness and strength from the ground up.

How to do it:

Lie face down on a mat with arms extended at your sides with palms down, and legs straight.

Engage your glutes, back, and shoulders.

Lift your arms, chest, and legs off the ground simultaneously. Keep your chin tucked in.

Pause at the top and hold for 5 seconds, then slowly lower back down.

Try This:

Try to count your breaths (at least 5). Then increase each time until you get to 10 breaths.

Move slowly and focus on control, not height

Why it Matters:

Strengthening the muscles along your backside (your "posterior chain") improves posture, protects your spine, and helps you move more confidently in everyday life.

This is a low-impact but high-benefit move, especially when done consistently.
