Making Space for What's Next

## Body
By now, you've likely felt some shifts, maybe in your energy, your mindset, or just how you approach your daily habits. That's not an accident. It's what happens when you start paying attention, even in small ways.

But here's the truth: making progress isn't just about adding healthy habits.

It's also about making space.

Space to breathe.
Space to move.
Space to choose differently than you have before.

It's hard to create lasting change when every part of your day is already spoken for. That's why the next part of this journey is about noticing what needs to be cleared out: the patterns, distractions, or expectations that might be keeping you stuck.

To help you with that reflection, I want to share a story with you. It's about one of my clients, Karen. She came to me in her 50s with chronic back pain and a strong belief that it was too late to feel good in her body again.

But it wasn't.

Karen's story is a reminder that you don't need more time, more motivation, or a perfect plan. You just need a small window of willingness, and the right kind of support.

📖 Read: Reclaiming Strength After 50 — Karen's Story

Let it encourage you to think about your own space.
What are you ready to let go of?
What are you ready to protect more fiercely?

Tomorrow, we'll talk about how to keep this momentum going. For now, just take in Karen's story and honor the work you've already done.

You're closer than you think.
