Feeling stiff in your back? These two gentle stretches support both thoracic spine mobility and lower back release — two key areas for feeling more ease in your movement and posture.

#### Part 1: Lower Back Twist

- Lie on your back with your knees bent at 90 degrees and your feet resting against the wall (or elevated on a chair).
- Extend your arms in a "T" with palms facing down.
- Engage your core by gently pulling in your abdominal muscles to support your spine.
- Slowly lower your knees to one side while keeping your shoulders grounded.
- Use a pillow underneath your knees if needed for support.
- Repeat to the other side.

Aim for 3–5 slow reps per side. Breathe deeply and let gravity do the work.

#### Part 2: Thoracic Spine "Open Book"

- Lie on your side with knees bent and stacked at a 90-degree angle.
- Rest your head on a pillow and extend your arms straight in front of you, palms touching.
- Slowly open your top arm in a wide arc, rotating your upper body and aiming to bring your shoulder to the ground behind you.
- If your shoulder doesn't touch the floor, use a pillow for gentle support.
- Flip and repeat on the other side.
- Rest and breathe into your belly in the "open book" and "closed book" positions. Practice the movement that feels the most tight.

Do 3–4 reps per side. Focus on opening your chest and breathing fully into each movement.
