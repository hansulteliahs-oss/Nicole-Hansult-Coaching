If you're over 40, strength isn't optional — it's essential.

I'm not talking about getting shredded or chasing a number on the scale. I'm talking about real strength:

- Getting up off the floor with ease
- Carrying groceries without strain
- Preventing falls and protecting your joints
- Feeling capable and confident in your body

By the time we hit our 40s, our bodies are already starting to lose muscle mass naturally. That means strength becomes essential for energy, mobility, metabolism, and confidence. Building strength isn't about lifting heavy or chasing perfection — it's about choosing resilience, vitality, and independence for the decades ahead.

Take a moment today to reflect: How are you building strength — physically, mentally, or emotionally? What small actions are you taking that reinforce your long-term health?

Now go back to your Focus Tracker. Look at where you've shown up this week, even in small ways. See the wins. See the consistency. Strength is built one intentional step at a time.

This journey isn't about doing everything perfectly. It's about not giving up — because your future self is counting on you.
