This move combines a classic glute bridge with a controlled chop motion — challenging your core stability, shoulder strength, and glute activation all in one. It's especially effective for cross-body integration and building foundational strength for everyday movements.

### How to do it

- **Set-up:** Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor, hip-width apart.
- **Bridge:** Lift your toes, press through your heels, and lift your hips toward the ceiling, forming a straight line from shoulders to knees. Engage your glutes and draw your navel toward your spine to hold the position.
- **Chop:** While holding the bridge, extend your arms straight above your chest and lock your elbows — the movement comes from your shoulders. In a quick, controlled motion, move your arms in a "chop" motion.
- **Focus on form:** Keep hips lifted and steady throughout. Your head and shoulders stay grounded. Don't forget to breathe.

### Targeted muscle groups

- Glutes (from the bridge position)
- Hamstrings
- Deep abdominals and obliques (from the chopping motion)
- Shoulders and arms (for stability)

### Repetitions

- Breathing slow and controlled, silently count 15 chops.
- Repeat for five rounds, increasing by 5 chops each round, resting briefly between sets.
- Prioritize form and stability over speed and reps.

### Why it matters

This move builds the kind of strength that helps you move better in daily life — especially when bending, twisting, or lifting. It's also a great way to wake up your core and strengthen your backside, improving posture and reducing low back discomfort.
