### Upper Body Rotation & Shoulder Range of Motion

This simple, wall-supported mobility exercise helps improve shoulder movement, thoracic spine rotation, and overall posture — all of which become increasingly important (and often neglected) as we age.

#### Set Up Your Position

- Face sideways next to a wall.
- Place your right knee and shin against the wall with your right foot forward in a lunge (knee above ankle).
- Your hips and knees should form 90° angles. Use a pillow or yoga block between your knee and the wall if needed for support or spacing.

#### Arm & Torso Movement

- Extend your right arm straight out in front of you, then start a controlled arc overhead, following with your gaze.
- Rotate your torso toward the wall as your arm completes the arc, allowing your upper body to follow the movement.
- Go slow. Breathe.

#### Repeat

- Complete 10 controlled reps per side.
- Focus on staying tall, steady, and relaxed through the movement.

### Why it matters

This drill gently challenges your mobility and control, especially in the shoulders, upper back, and core. It's not about pushing through pain or forcing range of motion — it's about restoring fluidity and ease where you might feel stiff or restricted.

You may notice one side feels tighter than the other; that's totally normal. With consistent practice, you'll feel more freedom in daily movement (like reaching overhead, twisting, reaching to the back seat of a car, or even sleeping more comfortably).
