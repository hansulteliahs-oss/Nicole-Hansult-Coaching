This sit-to-stand movement may look simple, but don't be fooled — it's a powerful exercise that challenges your core, legs, and balance. It's also one of the best indicators of strength and mobility as we age. The more consistently you practice it, the more ease and confidence you'll feel getting up and down in everyday life.

#### Instructions

- **Start on the floor:** Sit cross-legged on a flat surface.
- **Engage:** Brace your core and activate your glutes.
- **Stand up:** Without using your hands, lean forward slightly and rise to standing.
- **Control:** Move slowly and focus on balance and stability.
- **Lower down:** Return to the cross-legged position with control. Again, no hands.

Aim for 5–10 minutes a day practicing this movement. Small improvements each day add up!

#### Beginner Modifications

- Start from a chair and practice standing without your hands.
- Use a stack of pillows or a step stool to reduce the range of motion, gradually working toward the floor.

#### To Level Up

- Hug a light weight to your chest or do an overhead press as you stand.
- Increase reps or challenge yourself to move more slowly and mindfully.
